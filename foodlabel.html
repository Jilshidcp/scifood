Food Labels and its importance

Food Label provides useful information about food product to help us make healthier and safe food choices

Labelling information includes:
  • The Name of food
  • List of Ingredients
  • Nutritional Information Panel
  • Net Quantity-Amount of food/Drink without the weight of packaging
  • Declaration regarding Veg& Non-Veg
  • Name and address of the manufacturer
  • Date Mark
  • Country of origin
  • Instructions for use
  • Lot/Code/Batch Identification
1. The Name of the food

It is illegal for food to have false or misleading names or descriptions. The name of the food shall include trade name or must be a description which is meaningful enough to portray the nature of the food. If the food has been processed in some way, the process must be included in the title as
e.g.dried apricots, salted peanuts
• There are three types of names
1. Legal name 2. Customary name 3.Descriptive name

2. The list of ingredients

The list of ingredients on a food label must have a heading that includes the word 'ingredients'.In most cases, ingredients have to be listed in descending order of weight when the product was prepared.

Declaration regarding food additives

All the Food additives must be stated in the ingredients list by their functional name together with specific as PRESERVATIVE,Followed by either their chemical name such as MONOSODIUM GLUTAMATE or by their code number as (621)
Extraneous addition of colouring matter to be mentioned on the label as:
CONTAINS PERMITTED NATURAL COLOUR(S)
OR
CONTAINS PERMITTED SYNTHETIC FOOD COLOUR(S)
OR
CONTAINS PERMITTED NATURAL AND SYNTHETIC FOOD COLOUR(S)
• Extraneousadditionofflavouringagentstobementionedonthelabelas:
CONTAINS ADDED FLAVOUR

3. Nutrition Information Panel

Nutritionalfactsper100gm Containing energyvalueinkcal(kilojoule) and The Amount of Fat(saturated and total);Carbohydrates(total and sugars);Protein in grams in an average serve and 100 grams of the food.

4. Net Quantity

This is the Actual weight or volume of the product without the packaging. For canned foods and any liquid foods carry a declaration of the drained food of the product.It must be provided in metric units (kilos and grams or litres, centilitres and millilitres).

6. Name and address of the manufacturer

The label should contain the name or business name and address of the food business operator,packer,Distributor or Importer

7. Date Mark

The date mark of a food product tell us about the best time of eating by two ways

Best before
  • This date mark appears on most pre-packaged foods
  • Consumers can use the food after this date but the food may lost its best quality
    Examples; Biscuits, canned and frozen foods
Use by
  • Generally appears on perishable foods and short-life foods after this date the food is not safe to eat
    Examples; packed meat, chilled fish

The month and year in capital letters up to which the product is best for consumption, in the following manner:
“BEST BEFORE ....... MONTHS AND YEAR”
OR
“BEST BEFORE .......... MONTHS FROM PACKAGING”
OR
“BEST BEFORE ............MONTHS FROM MANUFACTURE”
But it is not applicable in the case of winesand liquors alcoholicbeveragescontaining10percentormorebyvolumeofalcohol.

8. Country of Origin

Country of origin is mandatory on a label if the name implies that the food comes from or has been made in a different country to where it was produced.

9. Instruction for Use
  • It is mandatory For the correct utilization of the product
  • It includes the manufactures instruction for preparing foods under the titles
    Preparation Methods
    Cooking Instructions

What is mineral water?

Everyone will have these questions in your mind at least once, Is mineral water really safe? Is it good to drink mineral water? How is mineral water different from normal bottled water?

What do you think? So let's clear it today.

Mineral water is water from a mineral spring that contains various minerals, such as salts and sulfur compounds. Mineral water may usually be still or sparkling (carbonated/effervescent) according to the presence or absence of added gases.

Then how is it beneficial?

Mineral water benefits are, they commonly contain substances like magnesium, calcium, sodium, and zinc, and, according to recent research, they’re actually a pretty effective way to boost your mineral intake. A recent study found that mineral water has some legitimate health benefits.

Then Bottled water?

Bottled water is drinking water (e.g., well water, distilled water, mineral water, or spring water) packaged in plastic or glass water bottles. Bottled water may be carbonated or not. Sizes range from small single serving bottles to large carboys for water coolers. By definition, this is bottled water from wikipedia. What do the numbers on the bottom of bottles indicate?

Friends actually, the number printed on the bottom of bottles indicates the type of plastic which is used for that particular bottle. Why am I informing you about this, that there is much false information on these indication numbers. So don't get confused.
One primary use of having these numbers is to help recyclers to sort into appropriate groups. In other words, identification number.And they are


  • ET, polyethylene terephthalate
  • HDPE, high-density polyethylene
  • PVC, polyvinyl chloride
  • LDPE, low-density polyethylene
  • PP, polypropylene
  • PS/PS-E, polystyrene / expanded polystyrene
  • OTHER, resins or multi-materials

Pest control in food and beverage industry

Pest management programmes in the food and beverage processing industry are increasingly influenced by food safety audits and govt regulations in recent decades. So it indicates that pest management in the future will pay heightened attention in every area of food industries.
On the other side,pests can contaminate food and transmit severe diseases. When pests appear in food processing plants, infestation can occur in a short period of time if they are not dealt with immediately. Implementation of pest control programmes prevents pest infestation. Pest control refers to the reduction or elimination of pests including mice, rats, flies, cockroaches and other insects.

Some important considerations to prevent pest include:
In your surroundings,
  • The surrounding of your plant should be free of debris and stagnant water.
  • Roofs and walls should be waterproof, because pests need water to survive.
  • Exterior lights should be placed away from the building to discourage insects from swarming near the building
  • Keep your doors closed whenever possible and must have screens to keep out insects.
  • Place inside garbage containers in a suitable location and ensure they are large enough to prevent garbage overflow.
  • Empty garbage containers at least once a day in a proper disposal site located outside your facility. Ensure outside containers are covered with a lid.
  • Flying insects can be controlled by using light lamps:
  • ➛    High voltage traps electrocute insects and can cause them to explode.Traps should not be placed within 9 meters of exposed food or food contact surface.
  • ➛  Low voltage traps have an attracting light and a replaceable,sticky board.
  • Eliminate cracks and crevices in the food storage and handling area. Cracks and crevices are the ideal areas for insects to hide and breed.
  • Food containers should be stacked on pallets. Leave enough space between stacks containers and walls for cleaning and inspection.
Use of Chemicals

Chemical pest control uses mainly pesticides and baits. Chemical used in pest control must not contaminate the food ,ingredients or packaging. All chemicals used for pest control should be properly labelled and stored in designated areas.

The use of pesticides can present a risk of product contamination, risk to the health of users and risk to the environment.For these reasons the use of pesticides will be a last resort. Where pesticides are stored on site, the store should be located away from food production and storage areas, kept securely locked and accessed only by authorized persons.

The use of insecticide baits is becoming increasingly common especially against cockroaches and ants. Insecticide baits have a low mammalian toxicity, making them safer to use where humans and other targeted organism are present.Some insects will return to their harbourages having ingested bait and after they have died their carcasses will be consumed by other insects, which are also subsequently poisoned. Baits are not suitable where a quick kill is required and are therefore usually used combined with other treatments.

Hiring a pest management provider

Food processors frequently use a licensed pest management provider. When looking for pest management provider, consider:

  • Expertise: a pest management provider should have a rigorous training in pest identification and behaviour
  • License: a pest management provider must be licensed by the concerned authority.
  • Proposal: obtain a full, detailed proposal about the scope of pest control procedure to be performed, it should include:
  • ➛    a service schedule
  • ➛    Trap layout maps of your facility
  • ➛    List of pesticides to be used
  • ➛    Response time for the procedure to be effective.
  • Report: pest management providers should supply reports with details of particular services, detailed sanitation and structural recommendations to avoid pest and corrective action required.
Conclusion

An effective pest management program in a food plant should include:

  • Routine inspection for detection focused on areas where pest are more likely to occur: receiving docks, storage areas, employee break rooms,etc
  • Analysis of possible factors that attracts pest, such as food residue, lack of cleaning, etc
  • Pest treatments that do not pose a risk to employees, food products or the environment.
  • Communication and cooperation between designated employees in charge of pest control and pest control providers.
  • Recording pest control monitoring activities to measure efficiency of pest control in case of an audit and to show areas that need more attention.

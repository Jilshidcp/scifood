Quality assurance vs Quality control

What is Quality assurance? and How is it different from quality control?

Quality assurance

Quality assurance is a set of activities for ensuring quality in the processes by which products are developed.It aims to prevent defects with a focus on the process used to make the product. It is a proactive quality process.
The goal of QA is to improve development and test processes so that defects do not arise when the product is being developed.It is very helpful in establishing a good quality management system and the assessment of its adequacy.It involves the prevention of quality problems through planned and systematic activities including documentation.
Everyone on the team involved in developing the product is responsible for quality assurance.Verification is an example of quality assurance.Statistical Tools & Techniques can be applied in both QA & QC. When they are applied to processes (process inputs & operational parameters), they are called Statistical Process Control (SPC); & it becomes the part of QA.

Quality assurance

Quality control is a set of activities for ensuring quality in products. The activities focus on identifying defects in the actual products produced.It aims to identify (and correct) defects in the finished product. Quality control, therefore, is a reactive process.
The goal of QC is to identify defects after a product is developed and before it's released.It is very helpful in finding & eliminating sources of quality problems through tools & equipment so that customer's requirements are continually met. It is a set of activities or techniques used to achieve and maintain the product quality, process and service.
Quality control is usually the responsibility of a specific team that tests the product for defects.Validation/Software Testing is an example of QC.When statistical tools & techniques are applied to finished products (process outputs), they are called as Statistical Quality Control (SQC) & comes under QC.
